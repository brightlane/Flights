Flight Comparison Sites FAQ (2026)

Which site shows the most airlines? ▼

Skyscanner typically indexes over 1,200 airlines, more than Kayak or Google Flights. This broad coverage increases your chance of finding budget carriers and hidden deals.

Skyscanner vs Kayak: which is cheaper? ▼

Skyscanner often undercuts Kayak on many routes, especially when using flexible dates or “Everywhere” mode. Multiple 2026 tests show Skyscanner with 10–17% lower average fares on certain itineraries.

Do I book on the site or the airline? ▼

Comparison sites like Skyscanner and Kayak redirect you to the airline or an OTA. For best support, book directly on the airline’s site when possible, using the deal you found on the comparison site.

Are flight alerts worth it? ▼

Yes. Skyscanner offers unlimited free alerts, while Kayak limits free alerts and often pushes paid tiers. Email or app alerts can drop your price by hundreds of dollars in 1–2 days.

Is Google Flights more accurate? ▼

Google Flights is strong for schedule and calendar‑view browsing, but it often misses many low‑cost carriers that Skyscanner and Kayak still capture. For maximum options, cross‑check with Skyscanner.

Can I trust comparison sites? ▼

Reputable comparison sites are safe when redirected to official airlines or major OTAs. Always check SSL (https) and avoid third‑party sites with no clear contact info or reviews.

Best for multi‑city or business class? ▼

Skyscanner has advanced multi‑city and flexible‑date tools, while Kayak is strong for structured multi‑segment itineraries. For business class deals, Skyscanner often surfaces more premium‑economy fares at economy‑class prices.

How fast is each site? ▼

Skyscanner is among the fastest, usually returning results in under 2 seconds. Kayak and Google Flights are similar, but some Expedia searches can be 2–3 seconds slower on complex routes.

Why spread searches across sites? ▼

Each site has different partnerships and data‑feeds. Running a route on Skyscanner, Kayak, and Google Flights means you see the full market, not just what one engine pulls.
