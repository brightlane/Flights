Pro Tips

How to Find the
Cheapest Flights

Insider strategies used by frequent flyers to consistently pay less.

01

Book Tuesday–Wednesday for Cheapest Fares

Airlines typically release seat sales on Monday evenings, meaning Tuesday and Wednesday often have the lowest prices. Use Skyscanner's flexible date tool to compare all days of the week at a glance.

02

Search Nearby Airports

Flying from a smaller regional airport nearby can save significantly. Skyscanner's "Nearby airports" toggle automatically includes these alternatives in your search results without extra effort.

03

Use the "Cheapest Month" Tool

Flexible on travel dates? Skyscanner's Cheapest Month view shows you the single cheapest day to fly in any given month — perfect for holiday planning when you have wiggle room.

04

Book International Flights 2–6 Months Early

The sweet spot for international airfare is typically 60–180 days before departure. Set a Skyscanner price alert well in advance, then strike when fares hit their lowest point in that window.

05

Try the "Everywhere" Destination

Not sure where to go? Type "Everywhere" as your destination in Skyscanner. It maps every accessible flight from your departure city by price — incredible for spontaneous or budget-first travel planning.

06

Split Tickets for Long-Haul Routes

Sometimes booking two separate one-way tickets on different airlines is cheaper than a single return. Skyscanner's "Hacker Fare" style results highlight these combinations automatically.

FAQ

Common Questions

Everything travelers ask before booking with Skyscanner.

What is the cheapest day to fly internationally?
Tuesdays and Wednesdays are typically the cheapest days to fly internationally. Airlines release fare discounts on Monday nights, so searching Tuesday morning often catches the best deals. Use Skyscanner's "Whole Month" calendar view to compare prices across all days simultaneously and pinpoint the exact cheapest date for your route.
How far in advance should I book cheap flights?
For international flights, the optimal booking window is typically 2–6 months in advance. For domestic routes, 4–8 weeks ahead usually yields the best prices. Skyscanner's price alert feature monitors your route and notifies you when fares drop, so you don't have to check manually every day.
Does Skyscanner include budget airlines like Ryanair and EasyJet?
Yes — Skyscanner is one of the few comparison tools that includes virtually all budget carriers globally: Ryanair, EasyJet, Wizz Air, Spirit, AirAsia, Frontier, Volaris, IndiGo, and hundreds more. Many traditional OTAs exclude these carriers, which is why Skyscanner consistently finds cheaper options.
Is Skyscanner free to use?
Completely free. Skyscanner never charges users to search or compare flights. You search, compare, find the cheapest option, and are directed to the airline's or travel agent's website to complete the booking — where you pay their price, with no Skyscanner surcharge added.
Can Skyscanner find last-minute flight deals?
Yes. Airlines often release unsold seats at steep discounts within 24–72 hours of departure. Skyscanner's real-time search captures these last-minute deals as soon as airlines publish them. For maximum savings on short notice, search flexible dates and consider nearby airports.
What is Skyscanner's "Everywhere" feature?
Skyscanner's "Everywhere" destination lets you search all flights from your departure city sorted by price — without choosing a destination first. It's ideal for budget-first travelers who want to discover where they can go for the least money. Simply enter your departure city and type "Everywhere" as the destination.
